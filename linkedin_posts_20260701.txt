1. COLLABORATIVE ARTICLE
==================================================
Google keeps losing its best AI researchers, and everyone is reading it wrong.

The names leaving are the kind you build a decade of headlines around. The person who shaped the core architecture. A Nobel winner. Two more senior people out the same door inside a week. Every time one of them walks, the story writes itself: Google is bleeding, the lab is in trouble, the lead is slipping.

That framing misses the actual lesson, and it is one that reaches far past Google.

If the people who trained the model can leave and instantly matter somewhere else, then the model was never the real asset. The weights sit in a file. Anyone with enough money can rent the hardware. What cannot be copied is the judgment inside those researchers' heads. Knowing what to test. Knowing where a system quietly breaks. Knowing which version is actually better when two look identical on paper. That travels with the person, not the checkpoint.

The same split is showing up in normal jobs right now. AI can hand almost anyone the polished output. It cannot hand them the taste to know if the output is any good, or the nerve to decide what to do with it. The work that felt safe because it required knowing a lot of facts is the work getting commoditized first. The work that felt fuzzy, judgment, direction, calling the shot when the data is mixed, is quietly becoming the whole job.

The people who stay valuable over the next few years are not going to be the ones who memorized the most. They will be the ones who built the judgment to tell a good answer from a confident wrong one.
